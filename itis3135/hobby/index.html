About Me

Why I Camp

I chose camping because I'm an Eagle Scout. Being outdoors has always had a strong place in my heart. I was in Scouting from first grade through my junior year of high school. COVID meant the overall end of my involvement with the troop, but I still find time to get outside.

I love the whole rhythm of a campout: the quiet, the crackle of a fire, carving wood, fishing, swimming, and especially camp cooking. Cold-weather camping is my favorite (yep, even 0° nights). Few things beat feeling cozy in a warm bag while the air is crisp.

Where (Best Locations / Places)

My Favorite Spots

North Carolina is a gem: I’m happy anywhere. Whether it's car camping, backpacking, hammocking, it’s all the same to me. Favorite regions include the Blue Ridge and the Piedmont. Always check permits, fire regulations, and Leave No Trace. Also note, some spots require reservations, especially in peak seasons. If you're fishing, also make sure to check local regulations, as well as make sure you have a fishing permit.

Top North Carolina Spots

  • Pisgah National Forest - Classic waterfalls & ridgelines
  • Linville Gorge - rugged views; be prepared
  • Uwharrie - closer to the Piedmont; mellow trails
  • Stone Mountain State Park - great sites & scenery
  • Blue Ridge Parkway campgrounds - easy access, big vistas

How

How I Prepare for a Trip

  1. Plan: check weather, maps, access/permits, fire rules.
  2. Pack: 10 essentials, warm layers, shelter, food, water treatment.
  3. Camp: pitch on durable ground, cook safely, store food properly, make sure you clean up after yourself.

Camp Recipe: Hobo Packs (Foil Packets)

Why I love them: easy, hearty, and perfect on coals or a grill grate. Especially in cold weather.

Ingredients (1-2 servings): 1 potato (cubed), 1 small onion (diced), 1 pack of kielbasa sausage, 1 pack of andouille sausage, butter, cajun seasoning, 1 squash (cubed), 1 zucchini (cubed), 1 can of corn, heavy-duty foil, pot for water.

Steps:

  1. Boil a pot of water, once boiling, add cubed potatoes until tender.
  2. While boiling, layer double sheets of heavy-duty foil, throw at least 1tbsp of butter in.
  3. Add veggies and cut meat; season well with Cajun seasoning; add some more butter on top.
  4. Once potatoes are mostly cooked, add those to the packet
  5. Fold one layer of foil and seal tightly
  6. Take the second layer of foil, place it over the top of the already folded layer. Making sure it's tightly sealed
  7. Cook 10-15 minutes per side on coals/grate (rotate once). Check doneness, be careful though, the steam is hot.
  8. Finish with a pinch of salt, and some more seasoning if you prefer. Eat straight from the foil with a spoon or fork.
