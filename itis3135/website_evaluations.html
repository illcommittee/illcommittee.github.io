Website Evaluations

Evaluating the effectiveness of website design through CRAP principles

Good Example: GKC

GKC is an incredibly well designed website, that follows modern CRAP principles with modern design, and an astonishing and smooth flow to their content.

  • Contrast: Excellent use of color contrast, text is easily readable and smooth on the eyes.
  • Repetition: Also has a very consistent layout throughout the entire website, fonts, colors, even spacing are all well-defined.
  • Alignment: A very unique grid layout that draws the eye to align text and items evenly.
  • Proximity: Items are grouped in a logical pattern that follows the path of the eyes, and improves readability.

Bad Example: Yale School of Art

While the Yale School of Art website has a visually creative design, it is incredibly disorganized and seemingly outdated. Navigation and readability are very compromised when viewing the site.

  • Contrast: A very busy background where the text, while easy to read, clashes harshly with the background.
  • Repetition: Seemingly no repetition throughout the site, and elements are inconsistent from page to page.
  • Alignment: Random elements don't really seem to align with anything, in fact, the whole web page feels skewed in one direction.
  • Proximity: Overall, related elements seem separated and visually just random. At least the elements are close to each other?
